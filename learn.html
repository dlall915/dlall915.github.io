Macronutrient

- a substance required in relatively large amounts by living organisms, in particular.

There are three macronutrients:

  • Carbohydrates: These should make up 45 to 65 percent of your daily calories and are made up of starch, sugar, and fiber.
  • Proteins: These should make up 10 to 35 percent of your daily calories. Their role in the body includes building, maintaining, and repairing body tissue. There are other roles too, including hormone production and in our immune systems.
  • Fats: These should make up about 20 perfect of your daily calories. Despite the misconceptions about fats, they play important roles in our body. Some of these are insulation to keep us warm and protect our organs, promoting growth and development, and helping to digest certain vitamins.

    • As you may know, alcohol also provides us with calories, but is not considered a macronutrient since we do not need it for survival.
